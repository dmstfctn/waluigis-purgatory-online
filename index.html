CHAPTER 1

A barebone checkerboard courtyard with two doors sets the stage for the opening scene of *Waluigi’s Purgatory*. As a floating head wrapped in a red cloak stumbles upon it - the protagonist AI, dreaming, or perhaps hallucinating - tickets fall from the sky. One reads: “Theatre of memory”, a term coined by the 16th century Italian polymath Giulio Camillo to describe an ideal architectural structure destined to locate and administer all human concepts - a world memory of sorts, storing everything which exists in it.

The stage dreamt by the protagonist then immediately reveals itself for what it is: a projection of its subconscious, a set of memories found deep in its embeddings, in the internal space of the neural network, and beginning to surface back.
